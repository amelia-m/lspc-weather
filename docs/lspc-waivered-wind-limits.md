# LSPC Waivered Wind Limits

Transcribed from the policy posted at the Lincoln Sport Parachute Club
(photo: [`lspc-waivered-wind-limits.jpg`](./lspc-waivered-wind-limits.jpg)).

> **All winds are measured in MPH and all gusts measured in the last 30 minutes.**

## Students

| Experience (jumps) | Max sustained wind (mph) | Gusts must be (mph) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0–5   | 0 to 15 | less than 16 |
| 6–10  | 0 to 16 | less than 18 |
| 10–20 | 0 to 18 | less than 19 |
| 21+   | 0 to 18 | less than 20 |

## Policy notes (verbatim)

> All excursion from the wind limits stated in the BSRs shall be approved on
> site by at least a USPA instructor before sending a student up in wind
> conditions higher than stated in the BSR. It is recommended that the S&TA be
> consulted if available.

> The Lincoln Sport Parachute Club is adopting this wind limit policy for the
> following reasons: the normal wind speed in Nebraska averages about 12 mph,
> students are given additional training on winds and canopy control above the
> FJC. The need for on-going education is paramount to the advancement of our
> students and the staff feels empowered to make our students better canopy
> pilots along the way.

## Notes for this dashboard

- Limits are **in mph**; the app converts to knots for comparison with METAR
  winds (which are reported in knots) and shows both units.
- The tiers as posted overlap at 10 and 20 jumps. In the app the tier selector
  uses the posted labels; pick the tier that matches your logged jumps.
- These limits are a **club waiver policy**, *not* USPA BSRs. The app surfaces
  them only as an advisory and links back to this transcription.
- **Re-verify against the current posted sign** before relying on these values.
